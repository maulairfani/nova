# Ad Slot Booking SOP — MCN TV

## Purpose

This document describes how the Sales & Traffic team books, confirms, and
manages advertising slots across MCN TV's channels (MCN1, MCN2, MCN3 —
national coverage; MCN Sport — regional coverage).

## Rate Cards and GRP-Based Pricing

MCN TV prices ad inventory the way the broadcast industry standardly
does: by **Gross Rating Points (GRP)** against a specific target
demographic segment (e.g. "Adults 18-49", "Housewives"), not a flat
per-slot rate. Sales & Traffic maintains a rate card per daypart per
demographic segment (price per GRP, in IDR):

- **Prime Time (19:00–22:00)**: highest price per GRP. Requires the
  longest lead time to book (minimum 14 days before air date) due to high
  demand.
- **Day Time (06:00–17:00)**: mid price per GRP. Minimum 7 days lead
  time.
- **Late Night (22:00–01:00)**: lowest price per GRP. Minimum 3 days lead
  time, and slots can often be booked on short notice.

An advertiser's campaign specifies a target demographic segment and a
**target GRP** to deliver over the campaign's flight dates (e.g. "600 GRP
against Adults 18-49 over 4 weeks"). Traffic allocates slots across one or
more airings/channels/dayparts to meet that target, using each airing's
trailing average Nielsen rating for the target segment to estimate GRP
delivered per slot. Advertisers may also buy against a specific DMA
(market) rather than nationally — MCN Sport's regional coverage exists
specifically to serve DMA-targeted sports advertisers.

## Booking Process

1. The advertiser (directly or via an agency) submits a slot request to
   Sales & Traffic, specifying the target demographic segment, target GRP
   (or a fixed slot count, for advertisers not buying against GRP),
   channel(s)/DMA, daypart, and campaign flight dates.
2. Traffic checks slot availability against the current schedule and
   responds with a confirmed slot plan or alternatives within 2 business
   days, estimating GRP delivery per slot from each airing's trailing
   average rating for the target segment.
3. Once the advertiser confirms, Traffic issues a booking confirmation
   and locks the slots in the schedule. Payment terms follow standard
   Finance invoicing (net 30 for existing advertisers, prepayment for
   new advertisers).
4. Creative materials (the ad itself) must be submitted at least 5 days
   before the first scheduled air date for compliance review (see the
   Content Standards & Compliance SOP).

## Cancellation and Make-Good Policy

If a campaign's delivered GRP (measured via Nielsen ratings for the
contracted airings/segment) falls short of the contracted target GRP by
more than 15% once the flight completes, the advertiser is entitled to a
**make-good**: additional slots at no extra charge, scheduled within 30
days of the flight's end date, sized to cover the GRP shortfall.

Advertiser-initiated cancellations:

- More than 7 days before air date: full refund, no penalty.
- 3–7 days before air date: 50% cancellation fee.
- Less than 3 days before air date: no refund; the slot may be resold by
  Traffic at its discretion.
