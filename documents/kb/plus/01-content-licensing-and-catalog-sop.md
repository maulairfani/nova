# Content Licensing and Catalog SOP — MCN+

## Purpose

This document describes how the Content Licensing team acquires,
onboards, and retires titles across both MCN+ products — the OTT
streaming catalog and MCN+ Shorts' micro-drama library.

## Acquisition Routes

Titles enter the MCN+ catalog through one of three routes:

- **In-house production**: streaming originals and Shorts titles
  produced directly by MCN+'s production arm. No licensing fee, but
  requires Programming Committee greenlight before production budget is
  released.
- **Licensed acquisition**: third-party movies/series (streaming) or
  micro-drama titles (Shorts) licensed for a fixed term, typically 12–36
  months. Requires a signed licensing agreement specifying the licensor,
  territory, window length, license fee, and simulcast rights (if any
  content is shared with MCN TV).
- **Co-production**: shared investment with an external studio. Revenue
  share terms are set per agreement; catalog ownership reverts per the
  co-production contract's terms at term end.

## Catalog Onboarding Process

1. Content Licensing Manager confirms the acquisition route and
   territory/rights scope for the incoming title.
2. Metadata team assigns the title's product classification
   (`streaming` or `shorts`), genre tags, maturity rating (SU / 13+ / 17+
   / 21+, per Indonesian KPI content classification), and release date,
   and enters it into the catalog system at least 5 business days before
   its intended release date.
3. For licensed titles, Content Licensing Manager sets a calendar
   reminder 60 days before the license term's expiry to decide on
   renewal, pull-down, or renegotiation.
4. Legal reviews and countersigns all licensing/co-production agreements
   before a title is scheduled for release — no title may go live without
   a fully executed agreement on file.

## Catalog Retirement (Pull-Down) Policy

- Licensed titles are pulled down automatically at license term expiry
  unless a renewal has been executed at least 14 days prior.
- In-house titles are retired only on Programming Committee decision,
  with a minimum 30-day advance notice posted in-app for subscribers who
  have the title in their watch list.
- Retired titles' engagement history is retained in the analytics
  database indefinitely for historical reporting; only the playable asset
  is removed from the catalog.
