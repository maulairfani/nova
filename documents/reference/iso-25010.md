# ISO/IEC 25010 (Reference)

Reference notes on the ISO/IEC 25010 product quality model, used for
[Section 1.2](../technical-design-document.md) (Quality Goals) of the
Technical Design Document. Source: iso.org / ISO/IEC 25010:2023.

## Big Idea

"Good quality" means different, often conflicting things depending on who
you ask — fast to a user, cheap to run for finance, easy to change for a
developer. Without a shared vocabulary, quality goals turn into vague
adjectives ("robust", "scalable") that nobody can verify or prioritize
against each other. ISO 25010 solves this by defining a **fixed, named set
of quality characteristics** everyone can point to, so a team can explicitly
say "we're optimizing for Reliability over Flexibility" and have that
trade-off be visible and debatable, instead of implicit and accidental.

## The 9 characteristics (2023 revision)

| # | Characteristic | Concerned with |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Functional Suitability | Does it provide the functions users actually need — completeness, correctness, appropriateness |
| 2 | Performance Efficiency | Resource use relative to performance — response time, throughput, capacity |
| 3 | Compatibility | Coexisting and interoperating with other systems |
| 4 | Usability | Learnability, accessibility, quality of the user interface |
| 5 | Reliability | Availability, fault tolerance, recoverability after failure |
| 6 | Security | Confidentiality, integrity, authenticity, accountability |
| 7 | Maintainability | Ease of modifying, debugging, extending (modularity, reusability, testability) |
| 8 | Flexibility *(replaces "Portability" from the 2011 version)* | Adaptability, installability across environments, scalability |
| 9 | Safety *(new in 2023)* | Preventing conditions that lead to physical harm or loss — mainly relevant to safety-critical systems |

## How it's meant to be used

ISO 25010 is a **checklist to filter, not a checklist to satisfy in full**.
The standard practice (also reflected in arc42 Section 1.2) is:
1. Go through all 9 characteristics and assess relevance to the system.
2. Pick the **top 3-5** as explicit quality goals — trying to optimize for
   all 9 equally produces no real trade-offs and isn't actionable.
3. Attach a concrete motivation/scenario to each chosen goal so it can later
   be tested (this is what arc42 Section 10, Quality Scenarios, formalizes).
