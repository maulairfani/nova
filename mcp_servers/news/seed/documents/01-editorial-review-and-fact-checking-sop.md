# Editorial Review and Fact-Checking SOP — MCN News

## Purpose

This document defines the review and fact-checking chain every article
must pass through before publication across MCN News's website, app, and
TV bulletins.

## Source Verification Requirements

- Every factual claim in a story must be attributable to at least
  **2 independent sources** for national/business news, or **1
  authoritative primary source** (official statement, court document,
  verified public record) for regional news.
- Anonymous sources may be used only for stories involving public
  interest disclosures, and require the Editor-in-Chief's explicit
  sign-off before publication — the reporter must disclose the source's
  identity to the Editor-in-Chief, even though it isn't published.
- Quotes attributed to a named individual must be verified verbatim
  against a recording or written record before publication; paraphrased
  statements must be clearly marked as paraphrase, not quotation.

## Review and Sign-Off Chain

1. **Reporter**: drafts the story and submits it with a source list to
   the Fact-Check Desk.
2. **Fact-Check Desk**: independently verifies each factual claim against
   the submitted sources, flags any unverifiable claim for removal or
   rewording, and returns the story within 4 hours for daily news (same
   day) or 24 hours for feature/investigative pieces.
3. **Section Editor**: reviews the fact-checked draft for editorial
   standards (balance, clarity, headline accuracy) and approves for
   publication.
4. **Editor-in-Chief**: final sign-off required for any story involving
   anonymous sources, legal risk (defamation exposure), or a public
   figure's personal conduct — all other stories may publish on Section
   Editor approval alone.

## Retention and Audit

Source lists, fact-check notes, and sign-off records for every published
story are retained for a minimum of 3 years, to support corrections
review and any legal inquiry.
