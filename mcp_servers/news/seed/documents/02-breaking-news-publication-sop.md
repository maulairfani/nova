# Breaking News Publication SOP — MCN News

## Purpose

This document defines the expedited publication process used for
time-sensitive breaking news, where the standard Editorial Review and
Fact-Checking SOP's normal turnaround times don't apply.

## What Qualifies as Breaking News

A story qualifies for the expedited process only if it meets both of the
following:

- **Time-sensitivity**: the story concerns an ongoing or just-concluded
  event where minutes of delay materially reduce its news value (e.g. a
  natural disaster, a major accident, an official announcement already
  public elsewhere).
- **Duty Editor confirmation**: the on-shift Duty Editor confirms
  breaking-news status before the expedited process begins — a reporter
  cannot self-designate a story as breaking news.

## Expedited Publication Process

1. Reporter submits a short initial draft (headline + verified core facts
   only, minimum 2 sources for the core fact) directly to the Duty
   Editor, bypassing the full Fact-Check Desk queue.
2. Duty Editor reviews for the same source-verification bar as the
   standard SOP but targets a **15-minute** turnaround, not 4 hours.
3. Once published, the story is flagged internally as "developing" and
   routed to the Fact-Check Desk for a full retroactive check within 2
   hours of initial publication.
4. Any fact that fails the retroactive check must be corrected
   immediately, following the Correction/Retraction Escalation SOP — a
   breaking-news designation does not exempt a story from correction
   requirements.

## Update Cadence

Once a breaking story is live, the assigned reporter or a designated
follow-up reporter must post a material update (new confirmed fact, or an
explicit "no update" note) at least every 30 minutes while the story
remains in "developing" status, so readers aren't left on stale
information during a fast-moving event.
