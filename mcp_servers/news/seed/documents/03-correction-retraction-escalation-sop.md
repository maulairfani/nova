# Correction/Retraction Escalation SOP — MCN News

## Purpose

This document defines how a factual error in a published story is
classified, corrected, and — where necessary — escalated and retracted.

## Error Classification

- **Minor correction**: a small factual error that doesn't change the
  story's core meaning (a misspelled name, a wrong date on a secondary
  detail, a rounding error in a figure).
- **Major correction**: an error that materially affects the story's core
  claim or a named individual's reputation, but the story's overall
  premise still stands once corrected.
- **Retraction**: the story's core claim is substantially false or
  unverifiable — the story cannot stand even in corrected form.

## Correction Process

1. Any error is first reported to the Section Editor responsible for the
   story (via reader report, internal review, or the Fact-Check Desk's
   retroactive check for breaking news).
2. **Minor corrections**: Section Editor approves and the correction is
   applied in-place, with a visible "Corrected on [date]" note appended
   to the article, within 4 hours of confirmation.
3. **Major corrections**: requires Editor-in-Chief sign-off in addition
   to the Section Editor, a visible correction notice at the top of the
   article (not just an inline edit), and — if the story already aired on
   TV — an on-air correction read during the next scheduled bulletin.
4. **Retractions**: require Editor-in-Chief sign-off. The story is
   replaced with a retraction notice explaining what was wrong and why,
   the original story is removed from active circulation (search/social
   promotion), and an on-air retraction is read during the next scheduled
   bulletin regardless of whether the story aired on TV originally.

## Escalation Timing

- Major corrections and retractions must be actioned within **4 hours**
  of confirmation for still-developing stories, or **1 business day** for
  older stories no longer receiving significant traffic.
- Any correction or retraction involving a named individual's reputation
  additionally requires Legal's review before publication of the
  correction notice, regardless of classification tier.
