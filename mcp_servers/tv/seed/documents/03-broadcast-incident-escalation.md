# Broadcast Incident Escalation SOP — MCN TV

## Purpose

This document defines what counts as a broadcast incident, the escalation
chain when one occurs, and the required turnaround time for incident
reports.

## What Counts as a Broadcast Incident

Any of the following, during a scheduled live or pre-recorded broadcast,
is classified as a broadcast incident:

- **Dead air**: loss of picture and/or audio for more than 5 continuous
  seconds.
- **Technical fault**: visible signal corruption, audio desync, wrong
  aspect ratio, or subtitle/caption failure lasting more than 30 seconds.
- **Wrong program aired**: the scheduled program is replaced, in whole or
  in part, by unintended content (including airing the wrong episode or a
  program originally intended for a different time slot).
- **Compliance breach during broadcast**: content airing without a
  required classification bug or disclaimer (see the Content Standards
  and Compliance SOP), discovered after the segment has already aired.

## Escalation Chain

1. **On-Duty Engineer**: the first responder. Must attempt to restore
   normal broadcast within 2 minutes of detecting the incident (via
   backup feed, ad insertion, or manual override) and log the incident's
   start time immediately.
2. **Broadcast Operations Manager**: notified automatically if the
   incident isn't resolved within 2 minutes, or immediately for any
   compliance-breach incident regardless of duration. Decides whether to
   cut to a backup program, insert a standby slate, or continue with a
   partial fix.
3. **Director of Broadcast**: notified for any incident lasting more than
   10 minutes, or any incident during a Prime Time slot regardless of
   duration. Has final authority on public communication (e.g. an
   on-screen apology or social media statement) if the incident is
   visible to a significant portion of the audience.

## Incident Report Turnaround

- A preliminary incident report (cause, duration, resolution taken) must
  be filed by the On-Duty Engineer within **4 hours** of the incident
  ending.
- A full root-cause report, including corrective/preventive actions, must
  be filed by the Broadcast Operations Manager within **2 business days**.
- Compliance-breach incidents additionally require a copy of the report
  sent to the Standards & Practices Manager (see the Content Standards
  and Compliance SOP) within the same 4-hour window as the preliminary
  report.
