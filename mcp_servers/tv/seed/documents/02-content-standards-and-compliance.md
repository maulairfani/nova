# Content Standards and Compliance SOP — MCN TV

## Purpose

This document defines how programs and advertisements are classified,
what on-air disclaimers are required, and the review/sign-off chain a
program must pass before it airs, in line with Indonesian free-to-air
broadcasting regulations.

## Content Classification

Every program and advertisement is assigned one of three classifications
before scheduling:

- **General (U)**: suitable for all audiences, no restrictions.
- **Parental Guidance (BO)**: may contain content requiring adult guidance
  for younger viewers (mild violence, suggestive themes). Restricted to
  airing after 21:30, and must carry the "BO" on-screen bug for the
  program's full duration.
- **Adult (D)**: intended for mature audiences only. Restricted to airing
  after 22:00, must carry the "D" on-screen bug, and requires a spoken
  disclaimer immediately before the program starts and after each ad
  break.

## Required On-Air Disclaimers

- Programs depicting dangerous stunts, extreme sports, or hazardous
  activities must carry the disclaimer: "Adegan ini dilakukan oleh
  profesional, jangan ditiru" ("This is performed by professionals, do
  not imitate") displayed on-screen during the relevant segment.
- Programs involving medical, legal, or financial advice presented by a
  non-licensed host must carry a disclaimer that the content is for
  entertainment purposes and not professional advice.
- Reality/competition programs involving prize money must disclose terms
  and conditions on-screen for a minimum of 5 seconds when the prize is
  first mentioned.

## Review and Sign-Off Chain

Before a new program (or a new season of an existing program) can be
scheduled, it must pass through the following sign-off chain, in order:

1. **Content Review Team**: reviews the full program (or a representative
   sample of episodes for ongoing series) against classification
   guidelines and flags any required disclaimers or edits.
2. **Standards & Practices Manager**: reviews the Content Review Team's
   findings, approves the final classification, and confirms required
   disclaimers are correctly placed.
3. **Programming Director**: gives final sign-off on the program's
   schedule slot, confirming the classification matches the proposed
   daypart (e.g. a "D"-classified program cannot be scheduled in Day
   Time).

No program may air without all three sign-offs on file. Sign-off records
are retained for a minimum of 2 years for regulatory audit purposes.
